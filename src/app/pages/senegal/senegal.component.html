Senegal

A good country to live in

A good quality of life

Senegal is a country with pleasant living conditions. The country’s sunny weather is refreshed by the 700 km of beaches which offer its residents and visitors a variety of leisure activities.

Shopping centers with famous international brands, gourmet restaurants, foreign schools (French, American) are helping to create a range of high-end amenities suitable for expatriates.

Entry and travel requirements are not complex for visitors as well as for residents. Sport, leisure and culture have a prominent place in Senegalese society.

Sport fishing, diving, sailing, Kart-racing and golf are some of the popular activities. The cultural life is rich and offers amateurs of high level local and international productions. Beachfront lodgings, wild life reserves and thalassotherapy centers invite you to relax and get away from it all.

Senegal has made significant progress in improving its transport infrastructure. Substantial investments have enabled the expansion of national and cross-border networks to improve the mobility and security of people and goods, and to promote the creation of new economic centers across the country.

In the road sector, major projects such as the Dakar-AIBD-SINDIA, Ila Touba motorways and the construction and rehabilitation of national roads projects will further improve access to major roads Dakar - Bamako, Dakar - Conakry and Dakar - Tangier.

Major projects are ongoing to rehabilitate and modernize the rail system in Senegal: the Dakar - Bamako railway and the Dakar Regional Express Train (TER).

Senegal has also put into service a very modern airport, located about fifty kilometers from Dakar. Blaise Diagne International Airport (AIBD) offers the very best in world-class airport standards.

Each African country has its specialties depending on its location. The proximity to the Atlantic Ocean makes the fish the main ingredient of senegalese gastronomy. It is thus cooked in different ways: pan-fried, braised, smoked, dried, etc. This allows Senegalese cuisine to offer different kinds of gourmet dishes.

Senegalese cuisine is considered to be the richest and most inventive in West Africa. This results from a rich history in cultural interbreeding (Arab and European influences from colonization by Portugal, France, etc).

Diners gather around the single dish and everyone helps themselves - with their (right) hand or a spoon. It is therefore surprising to note that the large bowl of dish is divided into as many parts as there are people around the meal. Many dishes have been created in Senegal, the main and national one is called Ceebu jen (Rice with fish). Just say “Thieb” and we'll understand you.

You must absolutely taste ceebu jen which is made with rice, fish and various vegetables.
These following dishes are also favorites in Senegal: Mafé, domoda, thiebou kethiakh, Kaldou, Yassa, thiéré (with sauce of your choice), Soupou kandja, thiou, lakh, fataya, Acara...

Democracy, multiparty system, solid institutions, recognition of political and social rights, freedom of expression: Senegal is an example in West Africa.

Senegal's location at the western end of the African continent in its most advanced part in the Atlantic Ocean, gives it a hub position connecting Africa to the rest of the world.

Praised as the easiest and fastest gateway to West Africa, it opens access to a common market of 300 million consumers.

Senegal also serves as an important gateway to major export markets in Europe, Asia and the Americas. Economic indicators have been improving steadily for several years.

Quality of human resources

Senegal has a large pool of young, skilled labor. It is estimated that the majority of the population is between 15 and 55 years old, and therefore is active.

Convinced that the quality of men and women constitutes the real wealth of a country, Senegal dedicates more than half of its national budget to education and health.

The country has many public and private primary, secondary and higher education establishments.

Prestigious universities and foreign schools offer high-level specialized training on site, or in partnership.
